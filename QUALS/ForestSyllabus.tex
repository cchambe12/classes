\textbf{\LARGE{Conservation Biology}}
\end{center}

\medskip
\textbf{Course Objectives:}
The goal of this course is for students to develop a basic understanding in ecology with an emphasis on forest structure and management. In this course, we will cover topics such as ecosystem dynamics, abiotic and biotic factors, trophic cascades, human impact and climate change, and population dynamics. We will meet every Monday and Wednesday from 11:30a - 1:00p.

\bigskip
\textbf{Resources:}
\begin{itemize}
\item Gotelli, N.J. {\textit{A Primer of Ecology}}. 2008. Sinauer Associates, Inc., Sunderland, MA (ECO)

\item Chapin III, F.S., Matson, P.A., and Vitousek, P.M. {\textit{Principles of Terrestrial Ecosystem Ecology}}. 2011. Springer Science+Business Media, New York, NY. (CMV) (\textbf{There are many copies available at the library.})
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Additional Reading:}
Additional primary literature articles will be available for students on the website and are listed in the `\textit{Reading}' column of the schedule below. Full citations are also listed. \\

\textbf{Field/Lab Sessions:}
Each week, students will have a lab session for two hours to learn basic ecology fieldwork and labwork. The aim is to provide students with a firm foundation in preparing for fieldwork, basic tool use, and greenhouse and growth chamber use. Students will need to first complete the lab safety module before the first lab session in order to participate. The module can be found on the course website. \\
